01

Your Wedding-First Strategy

Weddings are where the money is — and they're also the best marketing tool you'll ever have. A single wedding puts your work in front of 100-250 guests, most of whom are in the same life stage (engagements, milestone events, future weddings). Every wedding you serve is a live advertisement.

Why Weddings First

  • Highest ticket value — Your packages range from $800-$1,400+, and weddings consistently book the top tiers
  • Built-in referral network — Bridesmaids, groomsmen, and guests are your next clients
  • Vendor cross-pollination — Every wedding connects you with planners, photographers, DJs, and venues who can refer future work
  • Content goldmine — Weddings produce the most beautiful, shareable content for your brand
  • Seasonal predictability — Wedding seasons (spring + fall in Houston) give you a reliable booking rhythm

The Ripple Effect

One wedding done well leads to: 2-3 future bookings from guests who saw you work, a vendor relationship that sends you 3-5 referrals/year, and 15-20 pieces of high-quality content for your feed. Focus here first. Everything else follows.

02

Content Collection Checklist

Every event is a content opportunity. Here's exactly what to capture — even if it's just your phone. Set a reminder to grab these before you get busy pouring.

Before the Event

  • Clean, styled bar setup — shoot from multiple angles (straight on, 45-degree, detail shots)
  • Close-up of menu/signage display on the bar
  • Equipment and tools laid out neatly (shakers, jiggers, garnishes)
  • Garnish prep — cutting citrus, arranging herbs, filling ice
  • Full venue shot showing your bar in the space (shows clients what it looks like at their venue type)
  • Selfie or team photo in uniform, looking sharp and ready

During the Event

  • Action shots — pouring, shaking, muddling, garnishing (ask a friend or guest to snap these)
  • Signature cocktail close-ups with nice lighting
  • Happy guests receiving their drinks (get verbal permission first)
  • The couple interacting with the bar or holding drinks (the money shot for wedding content)
  • Boomerang/short video of a pour or shake
  • Wide shot of the bar with a line or crowd around it (shows demand)
  • Any special moments — toasts, first cocktails as newlyweds, etc.

After the Event

  • Screenshot or save any kind words the client sends via text or email
  • Note the venue name, planner name, and photographer's Instagram handle
  • Request professional photos from the photographer (offer credit/tag exchange)
  • Ask the client for a Google or Instagram review within 48 hours while it's fresh
  • Save any photos the couple or guests tag you in
"The best content comes from real events. Stock photos are a placeholder — your actual work is what sells."

Brand Voice & Visual Guidelines

Consistency builds trust and recognition. Every post should feel like it belongs to the same brand — elegant, warm, approachable, and professional.

Voice & Tone

Do

  • Warm and inviting — like talking to a trusted friend
  • Confident but not boastful
  • Professional without being corporate
  • Romantic and celebratory language
  • Short, punchy captions with personality

Don't

  • Overly casual or slangy
  • Pushy sales language ("DM us NOW!")
  • Negative comments about other vendors
  • Using excessive emojis (1-2 per caption max)
  • Generic stock captions ("Another beautiful wedding!")

Visual Identity

Photo Editing Tips

  • Warm tones — Increase warmth slightly, reduce blue tones
  • Soft contrast — Not too harsh, keep it airy and inviting
  • Consistent filter — Pick one Lightroom preset or Instagram filter and stick with it
  • Good lighting — Golden hour and warm indoor lighting photograph best. Avoid harsh flash
  • Rule of thirds — Off-center compositions feel more editorial and elegant

Caption Formula

Hook (first line grabs attention) + Story (2-3 sentences about the event/moment) + CTA (question or call-to-action) + Hashtags (in comments or after line breaks)

Example: "This is what happens when you let your bar be the centerpiece. Sarah & James wanted their cocktails to steal the show — and they did. From custom lavender margaritas to a whiskey bar that had the groomsmen cheering, this wedding was one for the books. Planning your wedding? Let's talk about your bar."

Outreach Targets & Partnerships

The wedding industry runs on referrals. Your goal is to become the bartender that every planner, venue, and photographer recommends. Here's who to build relationships with and how.

Tier 1 — High-Value (Pursue First)

Wedding Planners & Coordinators

Why: They're the gatekeepers. When a bride asks "Do you know a mobile bartender?", you want to be the name that comes up. One good planner relationship can send you 5-10 bookings per year.

How to find them: Search Houston wedding planner on Instagram, The Knot, WeddingWire. Look for planners who work in your price range (mid to high-end but not mega-luxury yet).

Action: Follow, engage with their content for 2 weeks, then send a warm DM or email (see templates in Section 7). Offer to do a styled shoot together.

Wedding Venues

Why: Many venues have a preferred vendor list. Getting on that list means automatic referrals. Some venues require outside bartending since they don't provide it — this is your goldmine.

Target venues: Boutique venues, ranches, estates, gardens, and non-traditional spaces (warehouses, rooftops) in the Houston area — these are more likely to need/allow outside bar service.

Action: Visit in person with a printed portfolio/lookbook. Bring a sample cocktail menu. Ask about their vendor list process.

Wedding Photographers & Videographers

Why: They're at every wedding and talk to brides daily. They also produce the professional content you need. It's a perfect mutual referral relationship — you refer them, they refer you.

Action: After working the same event, reach out to exchange content and discuss referrals. Offer to tag and credit them in every post that uses their photos.

Tier 2 — Strong Partnerships

DJs & Live Bands

They work every wedding weekend. Easy mutual referral — you share each other's cards at events and tag on social media.

Florists & Decorators

They handle the aesthetic side and often coordinate with other vendors. A styled shoot with a florist makes incredible content for both of you.

Wedding Cake / Catering

Caterers often get asked about bar service. If they don't offer it, they'll refer you. Offer a reciprocal referral arrangement.

Bridal Shops & Hair/Makeup

They see brides early in the planning process. A business card on their counter or a "vendor we love" post can drive leads.

Tier 3 — Visibility & Exposure

Wedding Expos & Bridal Shows

Why: Hundreds of engaged couples in one place, actively looking for vendors. This is where you can book 5-15 consultations in a single day.

Houston expos to target:

  • The Houston Bridal Extravaganza Show (largest in the area)
  • Weddings in Houston Bridal Show
  • Premier Bride Wedding Expo
  • Galveston Island Bridal Show (beach wedding market)
  • Local boutique bridal markets (smaller, more intimate, lower booth cost)

Booth tips: Serve sample cocktails (mocktails or small pours if allowed), display your bar, have QR codes to your website, collect emails, and bring printed package cards. Make your booth Instagram-worthy — brides will tag you.

Local Wedding Blogs & Magazines

Submit real weddings to Houston Wedding Blog, Brides of Houston, Style Me Pretty, and local lifestyle publications. Free press + backlinks.

Networking Groups

Join Houston wedding vendor Facebook groups, NACE (National Association for Catering & Events), and local small business meetups. Show up, build genuine relationships.

Online Presence & Vendor Platforms

Beyond your website and Instagram, here's where engaged couples actively search for wedding vendors. Being present on these platforms puts you in front of brides who are ready to book.

Google Business Profile (FREE — Set Up Immediately)

This is the single most important thing you can do for local visibility. When someone Googles "mobile bartender Houston" or "wedding bartender near me," you want to show up.

  • Create a profile at business.google.com
  • Category: "Bartending Service" or "Event Planner"
  • Add photos, hours, service area (Houston metro), and your website link
  • Collect Google reviews aggressively — this is the #1 ranking factor
  • Post weekly updates (like Instagram posts but for Google)

The Knot

Create a free vendor profile. Brides actively search here. Add photos, reviews, pricing range, and link to your site. Paid upgrades available but start free.

WeddingWire

Same as The Knot (they're owned by the same company). Having profiles on both doubles your visibility. Free tier is sufficient to start.

Zola

Growing wedding platform. Create a vendor profile and list your services. Many millennial and Gen Z couples use Zola for their entire wedding planning.

Thumbtack

Lead generation platform. People post "I need a bartender for my wedding" and you can respond. Pay-per-lead model. Good for filling gaps in your calendar.

Facebook Strategy

  • Facebook Business Page — Keep it updated with same content as Instagram. Many older clients (parents paying for weddings) search Facebook first.
  • Facebook Groups — Join "Houston Brides," "Houston Wedding Planning," and similar groups. Don't spam. Answer questions, be helpful, and mention your service naturally when relevant.
  • Marketplace — Some couples search for event services on FB Marketplace. List your service there.

Pinterest (Underrated)

Brides spend hours on Pinterest. Create a business account. Pin your event photos, cocktail shots, and bar setup images with keyword-rich descriptions like "Houston mobile bar wedding setup" or "signature wedding cocktail ideas." Pins drive traffic to your website for months or years — they're evergreen, unlike Instagram posts.

Referral & Review Strategy

Word of mouth is your most powerful marketing channel. Here's how to systematize it so referrals and reviews come in consistently — not randomly.

Collecting Reviews

The 48-Hour Rule

Ask for a review within 48 hours of the event — while the excitement is still fresh. Send a warm text or email:

"Hi [Name]! I had the best time bartending your [wedding/event] — your guests were amazing and the whole night was so beautiful. If you have a moment, it would mean the world to me if you left a quick review on Google (or Instagram). Here's the link: [direct link]. Thank you so much, and congratulations again!"

Pro tip: Create a short link (bit.ly or similar) directly to your Google review form. Save it in your phone notes so you can send it instantly.

Referral Program Ideas

Client Referral

Offer past clients $50-$100 off their next event (or a gift card) for every referral that books. Simple, effective, and your past clients are your best salespeople.

Vendor Referral

Offer wedding planners and photographers a referral fee ($50-$100 per booking) or reciprocal referrals. Keep track in a simple spreadsheet.

Social Referral

Offer a small incentive (free signature cocktail upgrade) when a couple tags you in their wedding photos or shares your page in a bride group.

Event Guest Cards

Place small, elegant business cards at the bar with "Loved our cocktails? Book us for your next event" + QR code. Capture guests while they're impressed.

Review Platforms to Prioritize

  1. Google (most important for local SEO — aim for 20+ reviews)
  2. The Knot / WeddingWire (couples actively read these when vendor shopping)
  3. Instagram (DM screenshots, story shoutouts, tagged posts)
  4. Facebook (Page reviews help with credibility)

Seasonal Content Calendar

Align your content and outreach with the natural rhythm of the wedding industry. In Houston, the two peak wedding seasons are March-May and September-November (avoiding summer heat).

Month Focus Content & Actions
January Engagement Season Boost Target newly engaged couples (holiday proposals). Post "2025 booking" content. Reach out to planners for the new year. Update vendor profiles.
February Valentine's + Bridal Shows Valentine's themed cocktail posts. Attend bridal expos. "Love is in the air" content. Push early booking discounts.
March-May Peak Wedding Season #1 Heavy event content. Post recaps every week. Collect reviews immediately. Network hard at events. Your feed should be full of real work.
June-August Summer Slow Season (Houston heat) Use this time to: update your website, do styled shoots, reach out to new vendors, attend networking events, plan fall marketing, create educational content.
September-November Peak Wedding Season #2 Same as spring — heavy event content, networking, review collection. Holiday party season starts in November. Start marketing private events.
December Holiday Parties + Year End Corporate holiday party content. "Year in review" post. Thank your vendors and clients. Tease next year's availability. Holiday cocktail recipes.

Key Dates to Post Around

  • National Margarita Day (Feb 22) — fun cocktail post
  • Valentine's Day — romantic cocktails, engagement content
  • Wedding Season Kickoff (March) — "our books are filling up" urgency
  • National Cocktail Day (March 24) — recipe content
  • Derby Day (May) — mint julep content, classy vibes
  • National Bourbon Day (June 14) — spotlight a whiskey cocktail
  • Engagement Season (Nov-Jan) — target newly engaged couples
  • Small Business Saturday (Nov) — share your story, behind the scenes
